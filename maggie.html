Philosophy of Education Video Analysis

Overview

This assignment offers you an opportunity to more closely examine five educational philosophies from your textbook and then apply them to a video case study. Keep in mind: teaching is usually a blend of one or more of these philosophies, and it is important as a future teacher to be able to recognize some of the characteristics of the philosophies in practice and how they impact classrooms, teaching and learning.

Step-by-Step Directions:

  • First, read over the following five philosophies of education in your textbook, and take notes about their principles and how they might be enacted in a classroom: Teacher-Centered Philosophies

    • Teacher-Centered Philosophies

    • Essentialism
    • Perennialism

    Student-Centered Philosophies

    • Progressivism
    • Social Reconstructionism
    • and Existentialism
  • Next, watch Video Segment #22: Essentialism in Action: A Classroom Lesson. As you are viewing the video, look for 5 moments that show evidence of one of the five philosophies. You can document evidence from only one or all of the philosophies. *You may need to watch the video several times to find your evidence.
  • As you choose your 5 moments, make sure to note the time that they occur in the video. Then, add each moment, and the time it occurs, to the graphic organizer included with this assignment.
  • Be sure to share a detailed and specific rationale for each of your choices. *Your rationales must be substantive, providing sufficient evidence to explain why and how these moments relate to a specific teaching philosophy.
